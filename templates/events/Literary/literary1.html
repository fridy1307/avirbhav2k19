आर या पार
(Hindi Debate)

There are two rounds . One includes preliminary round and the other one is final round.

Rules for Preliminary round :

  1. There is no restriction on the number of participants from each house.
  2. Each house will be given a particular slot for preliminary round.
  3. A topic will be given on spot and you have to speak for 2 minutes in favour or against of the topic.
  4. Top 8 participants will be selected for the final round.
  5. Decisions of the Judges will be treated as final.
  6. Complete rules for preliminary round will be posted on the facebook page of AVIRBHAV before start of the event.
  7. Rules for final round will be updated after the preliminary round.

Prime Time
(English Debate)

English debate will be organised in three stages:

1. Elimination Round - The extempore round,
Every participant will be given a topic to present on stage. The performance will be judged on the basis of the content of their speech, Confidence, Fluency and Pronunciation.

2. Turncoat and Q/A - Turncoat is a form of debate where the speaker literally debates against oneself. The speaker starts by taking a stance on the topic and switches sides after a specific duration of time.

Turncoat Debate rules and regulations:

i.) Topics: The topics will be given on the spot
ii.) Preparation time: 30 minutes after topic is given.
iii.) Speaking time: Each speaker will have to speak for 2 min 30 seconds on the given topic. The initial will be given to the speaker along with the topic. The order or changing sides is as follows:
A. 1 minute - For
B. 1 minute - Against
C. 30 seconds - Summary

In the end there will be an open Q/A round in which each participant is open to ask questions to the person on stage.
There will be marks for the quality of questions asked as well.

Judging Criteria:

40% Content
20% Physical presence - Eye contact, body language and poise.
20% Voice and articulation - Volume, pace, rhythm, intonation and proper pronunciation.20% Language - Appropriateness and accurate use of language.

3. Face off - Only 8 participants will advance in the Face Off round in which each participant will pair up against another participant and will have a verbal face off on a given topic. The time duration will be 8 mins for the face off round.
Winning an argument may not conclude you to be the winner, judgement criteria for the face off round will depend on the clarity of the argument, use of sattires, presentation style, ability to defend the argument.

P.S. - The face off has to be on softer note. Verbal abuse and offensive words are not allowed.

काव्यांजलि
(Hindi Poetry)

Go ahead n take your heart out....
Poetry is the best way to convey what you have in your heart….

Rules:

  1. There is no restriction on the number of participations.
  2. Participants will be given a topic, phrase, situation or aarticular category (patriotism, nature, romance etc.) and they have to write a poem on it.
  3. The poems can be of any genre like classic Kavita or shaayari (ghazals).
  4. The poem should be written in Hindi.
  5. Plagiarism is strictly prohibited and if found will lead to disqualification straight away.
  6. Total time of One hour will be allotted to write the poem.
  7. Few poems will be selected and they will be presented on the stage by their Writer.
  8. Judging will be based on the content of the poem, rhyme, use of figure of speech, issue being raised, fluency and confidence of the reciter and presentation .
  9. Judge’s decision will be final.

Words Not Spoken
(English Poetry)

Rules:

  1. It is an online event for English poetry.
  2. No restrictions in number of entries.
  3. Do not advertise any person or political agenda.
  4. You are not allowed to hurt the sentiments of any person, group, community or religion, violation will lead to disqualification.
  5. Use of abusive language is prohibited.
  6. Plagiarism is prohibited. Any participant found to have copied the poetry will be disqualified and marks will be deducted from the team.

War of Mighty
(Story Writing)

Story telling is an effective way to communicate. The art of writing has the ability to draw us in, to inspire us, to make connections and to make us equal, understood and whole. Everyone has a story to tell.
Avirbhav brings you an opportunity to tell your tale. Let your pen be your sword and words your armor in this battle.

Rules:

  1. This is an individual event. No restrictions on number of entries.
  2. Participants shall be provided with rough sheets.
  3. Participants are required to bring their own pen/pencils.
  4. Frame of story shall be given on spot.
  5. Time allotted to each participant is 1.5 hour.
  6. Your story should follow the frame given on spot. Story should have an introduction, body and conclusion. Plot twist in story is encouraged.

Organisers:

Sana Anwer

Open Mic

A mic for all, bringing beginners, amateurs & professionals under the same roof, sharing the same stage with their poetries, stories and experiences and competing for the audiences’ applause.

Rules:

  1. Maximum 10 participants are allowed from each house.
  2. Every participants will be allowed 5-7 minutes for his/her performance.
  3. The performance will be judged on the participants’ ”content” , “ confidence” and “fluency”.
  4. The content may vary from poetry to stories, from memories to experiences.
