The Black Walker

Winter is coming!

You are a secret spy beyond the wall, keeping an eye on the white walkers and army of dead. A few days ago you noticed that the army of dead is on the rise.

Your job is to alert people on the wall. You have exhausted all other resources. All that is left is your pet black horse (the black Walker). You need to reach the other side before it’s too late……

Event Description

The challenge of the competition is to make a robot that can move on black lines on a white background and reach the finish as soon as possible. The ROBOT which will start on the START tile and reach the FINISH tile block by following the black lines in the shortest time.

Rules:

  1. A team of at most 4 and at least 2 is allowed.
  2. Robot should fulfill the given description of size.
  3. Only one team is allowed to run on the track at a time.
  4. Calibration time will be given to each team before start off run.
  5. Event shall be concluded in 2 rounds. First is the qualifier round. Time shall be the judge for you. Round 2 or the final round details shall be disclosed later.
  6. Each team should get ready in given time for run, failing can cause disqualification.
  7. Human interventions are allowed but will lead to deduction of points.
  8. Bots will undergo authenticity check , the participant should not dismantle their bot before completion of any round.
  9. Hard coding will lead to immediate disqualification.
  10. Decision made by Organizer shall be treated as final.

Arena:

Black lines of width 3cm on white background. A sample arena has been shown below. The real arena would be disclosed at the time of the competition

Robot Specifications:

  1. Each team is allowed to have only one autonomous bot.
  2. The bot must fit into cuboids of 20x20x12 cm {Length x Breadth x Height}.
  3. The bot should not communicate with any other devices. Data transfer violation will lead to disqualification.

Race of Dragons

Avirbhav brings you the opportunity to test your efficiency and skills as young and enthusiastic robot designer. Brush up your skills. Prepare yourselves for a race to remember. It’s the time for the dragons to blaze and roar. Gear up your motors, coz it’s time to roll.

Event Description

Design a manually controlled ROBOT that has capacity to cover maximum distance in shortest possible time. There shall be two rounds – Qualifier and Finals. Qualifier round is race of 50m. Final round will be a challenging race of 100m.

Race your bots till the finish line and earn accolades.

Rules:

  1. A team can have at most four members and at least one.
  2. Robot should fulfill the given requirement of size.
  3. Round 1 consist of 50 m straight race. It will be a time-trail round.
  4. In case of a tie, a tie-breaker round shall follow.
  5. Human interventions are allowed with penalty of -10 sec.
  6. If robot goes off the track, penalty of -5 sec will be added to the overall time.
  7. The different teams qualifying for final round shall race for 100m. All qualified teams shall run their bot at same time. The fastest one will be declared winner.
  8. Details of final 100m race shall be disclosed on the spot.
  9. Unethical behavior could lead to disqualification.
  10. Judge’s decision will be considered final.
    NOTE - Rules are liable to be changed.

Robot Specifications:

  1. The maximum dimension of the robot can be 30x30x15 cm (l x b x h).
  2. The robot may be wired or wireless.
  3. The length of the wire (for wired bots) should be long enough to cover the whole track and wire should remain slack during the complete run.
  4. Max weight should be limited by 4 kg(including remote and batteries).
  5. Readymade toys car are not allowed.
  6. RC Engines are not allowed

Circuitous woods

Hold on your flags to encounter the victory by engaging yourself into the design challenge. It’s time to exhilarate the grey cells and put them for a test

Event Description

This event will be conducted in two rounds. First round (eliminative) tests the participant’s basic knowledge in electronics. This is a pen and paper round with multiple choice questions.

Round 2 is design challenge round. Qualified teams have to design circuit on PCB based on the lot they pick. Components to design the circuit have to be acquired by the team via auctioning using virtual money.

Rules:

Round 1:

  1. A team must consist of two participants only. No restrictions on number of teams from each house. Maximum participation is encouraged.
  2. Round 1 is eliminative round. This round will be of 1 hour.
  3. Time constrains is considered during evaluation.
  4. Use of unfair means will lead to disqualification

Round 2

  1. Each team has to select one problem statement from the given lot.
  2. Required components can be purchased via virtual money provided to each team.
  3. Output of circuit will be tested on CRO.
  4. Each team will be given 5 min prior auctioning of components to decide the components they require for the respective circuit.
  5. Each team shall be allotted 1 hour for designing the circuit and displaying the output.
  6. Each team is required to have own soldering iron and other required tools.
  7. Details of components and auctioning will be disclosed later.
  8. In all cases the decision of the judge will be final and binding

Code Battle

Code Battle is an exclusive event for quick coders who love competitive coding and love it even more when there is a timer running.

Event Description

This event consist of two rounds:

Round 1. Prelims : This is a pen and paper round (MCQ test) . It contains question based on basic programming (C/C++) . All the participants who qualify this round will be eligible for second round.

Round 2. Coding round: This is the final round of this event. It will be an offline coding round. It contains problems based on Basic mathematics, Data structures and Algorithms.

Rules:

Round 1:

  1. A team must consist of two participants.
  2. This test will be of 30 to 45 minutes.
  3. No restriction on the number of teams from each house.
  4. This round is elimination round.

Round 2

  1. Each team must bring their own laptops and accessories (if required) according to their convenience.
  2. Each team must ensure that offline IDE(Codeblocks, Dev C, .etc) is installed on their system.
  3. Allowed languages : C,C++,Python,Java.
  4. This round will be of 2.5 to 3 hours.
  5. Use of Internet is Strictly Prohibited.

The Watchers on the Wall

Time has come to hunt them down and save the Wall

Event Description

CTFs are computer security/hacking competitions which generally consist of participants breaking, investigating, reverse engineering and doing anything they can to reach the end goal, a "flag" which is usually found as a string of text.

Pointing Criteria:

  1. For submitting a flag points will be provided to the house
  2. Final Points will be (no. of flags from house)*5 + bonus
  3. A bonus of 25 points will be provided on submitting 5 flags.

Points to Remember:

  1. The event shall start on 17th April at 12AM
  2. You have a total of 48 hours to find the flags
  3. Any flags submitted after 19th April 11:59 PM will not be counted
  4. A Zip file containing ova file will be provided on website
  5. You have to download the zip and import it in Virtual Box / VMWare
  6. First house which submit the flag will only be provided points

Rules:

  1. All flags are of the format flag{passphrase}. For example, if you see flag{secret} on solving a challenge, submit only secret on the contest page.
  2. It is important that you submit the flags as you capture them. Don't delay the submission.
  3. Any attempts to DoS the contest infrastructure will attract penalties.
  4. If you find any weakness in the contest infrastructure, let us know without trying to misuse it. You may be given bonus marks for this.

Andro-war

Andro-war is an android application development contest.

Event Description

As we all know in this world full of conflicts, voting is a common method which is used to resolve this and arrive at any decision. So you have to design an android voting app.

Necessary features of this app:-

  1. The developer of this app (admin) should create a poll and add choices (minimum 4) available for voting to users.
  2. Other users should be able to login with their gmail or facebook account to this app.
  3. Users should be able to vote to their desired choices.
  4. Users should be able to see number votes to choices in realtime.

Optional features:

  1. Better UI.
  2. Users can add new options to an existing poll.
  3. Users can create a new poll.
  4. Displaying current voting status in graphical form.
Note: Application which have optional features will be preferred.

Rules:-

  1. This is an individual contest.
  2. No restriction on the number of entries from a house.
  3. App along with its code should be original. Coping code can lead to disqualification.

All the participants should submit apk file along with its code to Anurag chaudhary or Vishal Singhal.
Last date for submission : 11:59 PM, 17th April, 2019.

Quizzical Monsters

"Unlocking knowledge at the speed of thought"

Today’s modern life is surrounded with tech products. The technology has been growing rapidly for quite some time, and has now become an important part of life. It’s time to exhibit your technical knowledge and skills.

Event Description:

This event will be conducted in two rounds. First round (eliminative) tests the participant basic knowledge in computer science, electronics and quantitative aptitude. This is pen paper round with multiple choice questions.

Second round is stage round that further consists of three rounds.

Rules:

ELIMINATION ROUND –

  1. Each team would be given a set of question paper containing 30 multiple choice objective type questions
  2. Time limit - 60 minutes
  3. Only 8 Teams would be selected for STAGE ROUNDS.
  4. The selected teams shall have to appear for the final round.
  5. Use of unfair means will lead to disqualification.

FINAL QUIZ: STAGE ROUNDS

Round 1 : Tap Round

  1. A question will be fired & the team who tap the desk first get to answer first .
  2. 20 marks for the correct answer and negative 10 marks for the wrong or if team doesn’t after they tapping the desk. – No forwarding.

Round 2: Rapid Fire Round

  1. Each team will be asked 10 questions one after another.
  2. 10 marks for the correct answer and negative 5 marks for the wrong answer
  3. Answering time is only 5 seconds .
  4. Team discussion is allowed
  5. If a team cannot answer the question, they can say pass for the next question. The question will not be forwarded to the next team.

Round 3: Visual round

  1. Teams will be displayed picture and will have to answer.
  2. 10 marks for the correct answer and negative 5 marks for the wrong answer
  3. Each Team would be asked visual questions. ( 1 slide in each round )
  4. Answering time is only 90 seconds.
  5. No passing to the next team. Audience can answer.
